Ongoing Projects

Deep Learning for ALS Diagnosis

Using convolutional neural networks to classify ALS patient brain images and identify biomarkers for early diagnosis and personalised treatment.

  • Collaborators: University of Aberdeen, Prof Jenna Gregory, (Gregory Laboratory)
  • PI: Dr Marta Vallejo
  • Status: In Progress

Biomarker Discovery in Parkinson's Disease

Exploring machine learning models to detect subtle motor patterns from accelerometer data for earlier and more accurate diagnosis of Parkinson's disease.

  • Collaborators: Dr Juan Marin, Hospital Universitario Miguel Servet (Zaragoza, Spain)
  • PI: Dr Marta Vallejo
  • Status: In Progress

Health OpenSpaces

A year-long project based in the UK Young Academy with the question, “What would it take to change the underrepresentation of pregnant people in medical research?”

  • Collaborators: University of Edinburgh, Dr Andrea Ford and University of York, Dr Andrew Holding
  • PI: Dr Alistair McConnell
  • Status: In Progress

Completed Projects

Investigation of Retinal Imaging using Deep Learning

Developed state-of-the-art deep learning methods for fluorescence lifetime and retinal imaging in collaboration with Optos, enhancing early detection of neurodegenerative diseases.

  • PhD Student: Dorian Gouzou
  • Supervisor: Dr Marta Vallejo
  • Status: Completed (2024)

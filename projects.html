EPSRC IAA: Neuro BioMark ‚Äì Precision Biomarker Detection Platform for Enhanced ALS Diagnosis

This project aims to develop an advanced, accessible diagnostic tool for Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS). Using machine learning, the tool analyses neuroimaging biomarkers to assist in early diagnosis and patient stratification. RoboBarista: Longitudinal in-situ deployment

RoboBarista investigates how service robots can take orders and interact with customers, promoting a social connection within a public bistro setting. Broadly, this research project explores how social and service robot technologies can be seamlessly integrated into everyday environments, ensuring they are not just functional but also intuitive, inclusive, and beneficial to the communities they serve.

Investigation of Retinal Imaging using Deep Learning

RoboBrico: A Modular Assistive Robotic Platform Co-designed with Users

An EMERGENCE Network+ grant allows us to ask the question, can a robot help with hydration in a Care Home? The project consisted of three co-design workshops conducted with the residents, their families and staff at the Viewpoint Care home in Edinburgh as well as a robotic deployment of the RoboBrico Hydration platform.

  • Collaborators: Heriot-Watt University, Dr Mel McKendrick, Dr Mauro Dragone, Carol Pradeep, Konpanion, Camila J Pol, Alexandre Colle, Dr Scott McLeod, Dr Ronnie Smith, Old College Capital, Andy Coleman
  • PI: Dr Alistair McConnell
  • Funding Body: EPSRC - Emergence Network
  • Status: Completed (2024)
